## Future product direction — fit-constrained discovery

### Idea

There may be a broader Barefax opportunity around helping shoppers with hard-to-shop-for bodies or specific fit needs find products that actually work for them.

Examples:

- Larger-bust swimwear within a realistic price range
    
- Tall women’s jeans
    
- Very tall/slim men’s pants
    
- Long-torso swimsuits and bodysuits
    
- Petite/curvy fits
    
- Wide-calf boots
    
- Narrow-heel shoes
    
- Supportive bikini tops under a price cap
    

### Why it matters

Many shoppers are not just looking for “cute” products. They are looking for proof that an item works for their proportions, support needs, comfort requirements, or price constraints. Existing shopping tools often surface products, but not enough evidence about fit, support, reviews, construction, or whether a brand reliably works for that niche.

### Possible thesis

Barefax could eventually move from “is this item worth it?” to “what options actually work for people like me — and what proof supports that?”

### Relationship to current scorer

This should not replace the current Worth It scorer. The scorer should come first because trust and evidence-backed judgment are the foundation. Fit-constrained discovery could become a later product layer once the scoring/card experience is reliable.

### Open questions

- Should Barefax eventually support fit-specific search, such as “larger-bust swimwear under $150”?
    
- What evidence would be required to recommend products for a fit niche?
    
- Would this require review mining, product catalog indexing, brand fit reputation, or user profiles?
    
- Could the current card start tracking “fit proof” without becoming a full discovery product?
    
- Which niche should be tested first: larger-bust swimwear, tall jeans, petite/curvy fits, or another high-pain category?
    

### Do not build yet

Do not build full discovery, product search, user profiles, or recommendations until the scorer/card trust layer is stronger.
